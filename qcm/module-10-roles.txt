    MINI-QUIZ: Module 10 - Roles
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Question 1: How does Ansible determine a role name?

A) By the folder name
B) By the content of meta/main.yml
C) By the name in tasks/main.yml

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 2: What is the minimum structure of a role?

A) Just the tasks/ folder
B) tasks/, handlers/, vars/, files/
C) All folders are required

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Question 3: How do you use a role in a playbook?

A) `roles: - role_name`
B) `include_role: role_name`
C) Both are possible

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                        ANSWERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Question 1: A ✓
Explanation: The role name is determined only by the folder
name. If your folder is called `nginx`, the role is `nginx`,
period. The content of meta/main.yml, galaxy_info.name, or any
other file has NO impact on the role name.
Simple rule: 1 folder = 1 role = 1 name.

Question 2: A ✓
Explanation: Only the `tasks/` folder (with a main.yml file) is
strictly required. All other folders (handlers/, vars/, defaults/,
files/, templates/, meta/) are optional. Add them only when needed.

Question 3: C ✓
Explanation: Two valid syntaxes:
- `roles:`: static declaration at play level
- `include_role:`: dynamic inclusion in tasks
Both have advantages depending on the use case.
